Introduction

California Climate Initiative Fund (CCIF), generated from the largest carbon market in the United States - California Cap-and-Trade, are supporting a series of programs and projects statewide to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and deliver other economic, environmental, and public health benefits for all.

Here we will have a dashboard for user to explore some statistics and projects.

Pollution and Investment

Different from equity planning with the core goal to achieve social equity, the process of carbon trade-investment, however, focuses on equity from a reparative view. One of the most important legal foundations of carbon trade is that the credits paid by polluting installations for carbon credits are used to correct the negative externalities caused by pollution. The negative externality has the greatest impact on the surrounding communities of polluting installations spatially and should receive more "compensation".

There will be a parallel coordinates plot with climate risk index, pollution index, and climate investment intensity in each community. This plot can select same community with following one plot.

Socioeconomic Spatial Mismatch

Construct the spatial mismatch index based on different combination of socioeconomic variables.

There will be an interative map to show the different spatial mismatch index. I wish the user can control which socioeconomic variables they wish to use for the index. This plot can select same community with last one.
