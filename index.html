Bio

I'm a Ph.D. student in the Building Energy Technology Laboratory (BETlab) within the Ph.D. in Design Program at North Carolina State University (NC STATE). I also serve as the president of the NC STATE Chapter of the American Society of Heating, Refrigerating, and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE). Additionally, I am a Fellow in the KIETS Climate Leaders Program, where I contribute to interdisciplinary leadership teams focused on developing solutions that mitigate and adapt to the challenges of climate change. I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Soolyeon Cho. I am also the Principal Investigator (PI) of a research project supported by the Next-Generation Science & Technology (S&T) Leaders NET, Korean Federation of Science & Technology Societies (KOFST). This project focuses on a synthetic study of the energy potential of solar power systems at the community level, utilizing AI and spatiotemporal data.
