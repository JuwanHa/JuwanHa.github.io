Bio

I'm a Ph.D. student in the Building Energy Technology Laboratory (BETlab) within the Ph.D. in Design Program at North Carolina State University (NC STATE). I also serve as the president of the NC STATE Chapter of the American Society of Heating, Refrigerating, and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE). Additionally, I am a Fellow in the KIETS Climate Leaders Program, where I contribute to interdisciplinary leadership teams focused on developing solutions that mitigate and adapt to the challenges of climate change. I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Soolyeon Cho. I also completed a summer internship with the Building Technology & Urban Systems (BTUS) Division at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL) under the guidance of Dr. Donghun Kim, where I developed an AI-tech assistant leveraging Large Language Models (LLM) to accelerate the deployment of low-cost model predictive control (MPC) technology for grid-interactive efficient buildings (GEBs). I am also the Principal Investigator (PI) of a research project supported by the Next-Generation Science & Technology (S&T) Leaders NET, Korean Federation of Science & Technology Societies (KOFST). This project focuses on a synthetic study of the energy potential of solar power systems at the community level, utilizing AI and spatiotemporal data. Check my CV here.

I am deeply interested in implementing microgrids at the community-level using grid-edge resources such as Grid-Interactive Efficient Buildings (GEBs) and Electric Vehicles (EVs). My resarch focuses on developing Model Predictive Coordinated Control (MPCC) technology for building Heating, Ventilation, and Air-Conditioning (HVAC) systems by incorporating Distributed Energy Resources (DERs) such as Thermal Energy Storage (TES), Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS), Vehicle-to-Building (V2B), and Building Envelope as Virtual Batteries. This approach aims to enhance the energy flexibility of buildings, reducing peak loads. Also, I aim to develop technologies that support right-sizing decisions for DERs at the community level and improve resiliency, facilitating the implementation of microgrids within communities.
